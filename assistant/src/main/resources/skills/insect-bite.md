For a mosquito or insect bite:

- Wash the area gently with soap and water.
- Use a cool compress for itching or swelling.
- Try not to scratch, because scratching can irritate the skin and increase infection risk.
- Keep the area clean and watch it over the next day or two.

Consider speaking with a pharmacist or clinician about itch relief if it is very uncomfortable, especially for a child, pregnancy, allergies, or other medical conditions.

Recommend medical care urgently if there is trouble breathing, swelling of the lips/face/throat, dizziness, widespread hives, fever, rapidly spreading redness, severe pain, pus, red streaks, or the bite area becomes much worse.

Do not diagnose infection or allergy with certainty. Encourage a licensed clinician if symptoms are concerning, persistent, or worsening.
