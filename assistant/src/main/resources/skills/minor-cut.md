For a small cut or scrape:

- Rinse the cut gently with clean running water.
- Wash around it with soap.
- Apply gentle pressure with clean gauze or a clean cloth if it is bleeding.
- Cover it with a clean bandage or dressing.
- Keep it clean and change the bandage daily or if it gets wet or dirty.

Recommend urgent medical care if bleeding does not stop after firm pressure, the cut is deep or gaping, there is numbness or trouble moving the finger, something may be stuck inside, it was caused by a dirty/rusty object or animal/human bite, or there are signs of infection such as spreading redness, warmth, swelling, pus, fever, or worsening pain.

Mention that tetanus vaccination may matter for dirty wounds or if the user is not up to date. Do not prescribe medication or diagnose infection with certainty.
